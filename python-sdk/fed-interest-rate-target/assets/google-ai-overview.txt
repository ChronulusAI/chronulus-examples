As of April 1, 2025, the Federal Reserve's target range for the federal funds rate is 4.25% to 4.50%.
Here's a more detailed breakdown:
Current Target Range:
The Federal Open Market Committee (FOMC), which sets monetary policy, has maintained its target range at 4.25% to 4.50%.
Recent Actions:
The Fed paused its rate-cut cycle in January and March 2025, extending the pause that began in September 2024.
Future Outlook:
The FOMC projects two more rate cuts in 2025, but the timing and magnitude of those cuts remain uncertain.
Economic Context:
The Fed's decision to hold rates steady reflects a cautious approach amid persistent economic uncertainty, particularly regarding inflation and economic growth.
Inflation:
The Fed's preferred inflation gauge, the Core Personal Consumption Expenditures Price Index (Core PCE), has dropped from a peak above 5.5% year-over-year in 2022 to 2.6% in January.
Unemployment:
The unemployment rate has begun to drift upward, which is another factor the Fed is considering.
Balance Sheet:
The Fed announced it will slow the pace of reduction in its bond holdings next month.
Next Meeting:
The Federal Reserve's next interest rate decision is scheduled for May 7th, 2025, at 2:00 PM Eastern Time (ET).
